Robert Frost employs various literary devices in "Stopping by Woods on a Snowy Evening" to enhance its themes of nature's allure, duty, contemplation, and life's journey. Here are some of the key literary devices he uses:

1. **Imagery**:
   - Frost uses vivid imagery to paint the scene of the woods filled with snow. Phrases like "lovely, dark and deep" evoke the enchanting and serene beauty of the natural setting, drawing readers into the scene and highlighting the allure of nature.

2. **Repetition**:
   - The repetition of the line "And miles to go before I sleep" emphasizes the sense of duty and the journey ahead. It reinforces the weight of the responsibilities that the speaker must attend to, underscoring the contrast between desire and obligation.

3. **Rhyme Scheme**:
   - The poem follows an AABA rhyme scheme in each stanza, which creates a harmonious and melodic quality that mirrors the peacefulness of the woods. The rhyme scheme contributes to the poem's reflective tone and helps maintain its fluidity.

4. **Symbolism**:
   - The woods symbolize the beauty and mystery of nature as well as the temptation to escape from responsibilities. The journey and promises represent life's duties and commitments. The "sleep" at the end of the poem can symbolize both rest and death, suggesting the inevitability of life's journey.

5. **Personification**:
   - The personification of elements like the woods and the snow adds depth to the natural setting, giving it a sense of life and presence beyond mere landscape. This enhances the theme of nature's allure.

6. **Sound Devices**:
   - Frost's use of alliteration and consonance contributes to the musical quality of the poem. Phrases like "sound's the sweep" and "whisper of falling snow" create a soothing auditory effect that echoes the tranquility of the woods.

Overall, Robert Frost's use of these literary devices enriches the poem, allowing readers to engage deeply with its themes and appreciate the duality of human experience captured in the simple yet profound encounter with nature.

Agent:
Common themes in human dreams often include:

1. **Being Chased**: This is a frequent motif across many cultures and is often linked to feelings of anxiety and fear.
2. **Falling**: Often associated with a loss of control or fear of failure.
3. **Flying**: Can represent a sense of freedom or escaping from constraints.
4. **Taking Exams**: Often linked to stress and performance anxiety.
5. **Being Naked in Public**: Usually signifies vulnerability or fear of being exposed.
6. **Teeth Falling Out**: This is sometimes related to concerns about appearance or aging.

Dreams can vary significantly across different cultures or demographics:

- **Cultural Variations**: Cultural beliefs and practices can greatly influence dream content. For instance, some cultures place a strong emphasis on ancestral spirits and may have dreams involving ancestors or spiritual guides. In other cultures, dreams about animals might be more common, especially if animals play a significant role in daily life or mythology.

- **Demographic Differences**: Age, gender, and life experiences can shape dreams. Children often have more fantastical dreams as their imaginations are highly active, while adults might have dreams more centered around work, relationships, or personal fears. Gender can also influence themes; for instance, studies have shown that women might dream more about family and relationships, whereas men might have more dreams involving aggression or outdoor settings.

Understanding dreams often requires considering the personal and cultural context, as well as individual emotions and experiences. While certain themes may be universally recognized, their interpretation can be deeply personal and culturally specific.
